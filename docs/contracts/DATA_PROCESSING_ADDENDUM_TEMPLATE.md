# Data Processing Addendum Draft

This is a customer-review-ready draft for procurement and legal review.

It is a starting point only and must be completed, negotiated, and approved by counsel before signature.

## 1. Parties and Relationship

This Data Processing Addendum (`DPA`) is entered into between:

- `[Filed Legal Entity Name]` (`Processor`) (`use "dba Desir Solutions" only if the filed legal name differs from the public brand`)
- `[Customer Legal Name]` (`Customer`)

This DPA supplements the applicable Master Services Agreement, Order Form, and Statement of Work between the parties. If there is a conflict between this DPA and another agreement, this DPA controls only with respect to personal-data processing obligations.

## 2. Roles

- Customer acts as the controller or business for customer personal data processed under the services.
- Desir Solutions acts as the processor or service provider for customer personal data processed on Customer's behalf.
- Desir Solutions may act as an independent controller for its own business records, billing records, security logs, and compliance records where permitted by law and the governing agreement.

## 3. Subject Matter, Duration, Nature, and Purpose

### Subject Matter

Desir Solutions will process customer personal data only as needed to perform the contracted consulting, staffing, automation, support, and platform services described in the governing agreements.

### Duration

This DPA remains in effect for as long as Desir Solutions processes customer personal data on Customer's behalf under the governing agreement.

### Nature and Purpose

Processing may include:

- intake and CRM record management
- project, delivery, and support coordination
- proposal and workflow support
- service monitoring, security operations, and incident response
- supervised AI-assisted internal workflows when explicitly enabled by the service scope

## 4. Categories of Data Subjects and Personal Data

Categories of data subjects may include:

- customer employees
- customer contractors
- prospective customer contacts
- authorized representatives and project stakeholders

Categories of personal data may include:

- name
- business contact details
- employer, role, and business unit
- communication history and project records
- operational metadata relevant to support, security, and delivery
- any additional categories specifically identified in an Order Form or Statement of Work

## 5. Customer Instructions

Desir Solutions will process customer personal data only:

- on documented instructions from Customer
- as required to perform the services under the governing agreement
- as required by applicable law, in which case Desir Solutions will notify Customer unless legally prohibited

Customer is responsible for the accuracy, lawfulness, and completeness of the instructions it provides.

## 6. Processor Obligations

Desir Solutions will:

- limit processing to the purposes described in the governing agreement and Customer's lawful instructions
- restrict access to authorized personnel with a business need to know
- require personnel with access to customer personal data to protect it under confidentiality obligations
- implement reasonable technical and organizational safeguards appropriate to the risk
- maintain records, policies, or operational evidence reasonably necessary to support customer diligence requests
- notify Customer of confirmed security incidents affecting customer personal data in accordance with this DPA and the governing agreement
- assist Customer with reasonable requests relating to data subject rights, deletion, correction, or regulatory inquiries where commercially reasonable and legally required

## 7. Personnel Confidentiality and Access Control

Desir Solutions will ensure that personnel with access to customer personal data:

- are informed of the confidential nature of the data
- receive access appropriate to their role
- are subject to confidentiality obligations
- are removed from access when no longer required for the services

## 8. Security Measures

Desir Solutions will maintain safeguards appropriate to the nature of the services and the risks presented, including as applicable:

- named-user access controls
- role-based access restrictions
- approval gates for protected workflow write-backs
- backup and restore-drill procedures
- logging, incident tracking, and operational alerting
- patching and credential-management procedures

The current operational summary is described in `../security/SECURITY_OVERVIEW.md`. Customer-specific security schedules may be attached separately and will control if expressly incorporated into the governing agreement.

## 9. Subprocessors

Desir Solutions may use subprocessors to support delivery of the services.

Desir Solutions will:

- maintain a current subprocessor list
- remain responsible for subprocessor performance to the extent required by law or contract
- require subprocessors to protect customer personal data through written obligations appropriate to the services provided

The current operating-model subprocessor summary is documented in `../security/SUBPROCESSORS.md`.

## 10. International Transfers

If customer personal data is transferred across borders, the parties will document the applicable transfer mechanism in the governing agreement, an order form, or an attached transfer appendix.

## 11. Security Incident Notification

If Desir Solutions confirms a security incident affecting customer personal data, Desir Solutions will notify Customer without undue delay and in accordance with the notification standard stated in the governing agreement or applicable law.

Initial notice should include, where known:

- the general nature of the incident
- affected systems or services
- known or suspected categories of personal data involved
- current containment or remediation status
- a contact path for follow-up

## 12. Assistance with Data Subject Requests and Regulatory Inquiries

Taking into account the nature of the processing, Desir Solutions will provide commercially reasonable assistance to Customer with:

- requests for access, correction, deletion, or restriction
- regulatory or supervisory inquiries related to the processing performed under the services
- security reviews or factual diligence requests relating to Desir Solutions' role as processor

## 13. Return and Deletion

Upon termination of the applicable services, Desir Solutions will return or delete customer personal data within a commercially reasonable period unless retention is required by law, security, backup integrity, dispute preservation, or contractual recordkeeping obligations.

## 14. Audit and Information Rights

Customer may request information reasonably necessary to confirm Desir Solutions' compliance with this DPA, subject to:

- reasonable notice
- appropriate confidentiality protections
- non-disruptive timing and scope
- reimbursement of Desir Solutions' reasonable costs for extensive or repeated reviews unless otherwise required by law or contract

On-site audits should be limited to circumstances where written responses and existing documentation are insufficient and should be coordinated in good faith.

## 15. Liability and Order of Precedence

Liability arising under this DPA will be governed by the liability provisions of the governing agreement unless the parties expressly agree otherwise in writing.

If there is a conflict in processing-related terms, the order of precedence is:

1. executed amendment or order form addressing data protection
2. this DPA
3. the applicable Statement of Work
4. the Master Services Agreement
