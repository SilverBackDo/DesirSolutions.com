# Desir Solutions Incident Notification Policy

This document describes the baseline incident-notification approach Desir Solutions intends to use for customer-impacting security events.

It is a policy statement and template baseline, not a substitute for customer-specific contract language.

## Scope

This policy applies to confirmed security incidents that materially affect:

- customer data entrusted to Desir Solutions
- service availability for contracted services
- integrity of customer-facing deliverables or protected workflow actions

## Notification Standard

Desir Solutions will use commercially reasonable efforts to notify an affected customer without undue delay after confirming a material security incident affecting that customer.

Target baseline:

- initial notice within 72 hours of confirmation for material customer-data incidents, unless delayed by law-enforcement request, legal restriction, or incomplete facts that would make the notice misleading

## Initial Notice Content

The initial notice should include, when known:

- general nature of the incident
- affected systems or services
- known or suspected data categories involved
- current containment status
- immediate customer actions, if any
- point of contact for ongoing updates

## Follow-Up Updates

Desir Solutions should provide follow-up updates as material facts are confirmed, including:

- containment progress
- root-cause status
- remediation actions
- restoration status
- post-incident prevention actions when appropriate

## Contact Path

Default notification channels:

- customer-designated business contact
- customer-designated security or legal contact when available

Desir Solutions contact addresses:

- `privacy@desirsolutions.com`
- `legal@desirsolutions.com`

## Internal Trigger Conditions

This policy is intended to be activated when a confirmed incident involves:

- unauthorized access to customer data
- confirmed loss of confidentiality, integrity, or availability with customer impact
- unauthorized workflow write-back with business impact
- confirmed backup or recovery failure during a customer-impacting event

## Exclusions

This policy does not require external notice for:

- isolated unsuccessful attacks without confirmed compromise
- internal test events
- incidents confined to non-customer demo or staging data

## Review

Review this policy after:

- a material security incident
- a hosting or provider change
- a customer contract requiring a different notification timeline
